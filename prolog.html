Autumn in April

You brought autumn in my spring

March arrived like the soft whisper of spring,
its warmth brushing gently against the frost of my heart.
Everything began to bloom,
like hopes unfolding shyly beneath the tender light of a new season.

But on the tenth of April,
you came like an autumn wind
in the middle of my still-blooming spring.
And just like that,
My hopes began to fall,
quietly, gracefully,
like leaves drifting down without a sound,
touching the ground before I could hold them close.

I never asked for the beauty of falling.
I only wished the leaves would stay,
clinging tightly to the branches,
so I could rest a little longer
beneath the shade of the promises we planted.

Why did you bring autumn into my spring?
Couldn’t you let one season last
until I was ready to let it go?

At the very least…
let me breathe in my spring
before it all turns into a goodbye I never asked for.

And maybe, just maybe… this is where all my sorrow began.
