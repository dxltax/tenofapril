Still, I’d Wait for You in the Falling

There is no voice loud enough,
no wisdom kind enough,
to pull me out of the madness
that loving you became.

Even if you drift in and out
like the changing winds of autumn,
uncertain,
untouchable,
as if I were nothing more than a leaf caught in your passing,
trust me…
I’d still be standing here,
arms open,
a tree stripped bare,
begging the wind to return
even if it only ever takes.

No matter how deep the ache,
you are the wound
I’d let reopen,
again and again,
the one I cradle like brittle leaves
I refuse to let go.

And if, by some twist of fate,
your eyes ever rest upon these words,
know this…
