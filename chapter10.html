Let Me Be the Leaf That Loved You

I’ve spent too many dusks
blaming myself,
for all the branches I couldn't hold onto,
for every promise
that fell like early leaves in October.

I hated myself
for not being the season
that could make you stay.
But I would hate myself more
if even my letting go
failed to keep you warm
through your coming cold.

So promise me this—
beneath the amber sky,
where the wind listens
but never speaks back…

Promise you'll still bloom,
even after my shade is gone.
Promise you'll love your own skin
more than you ever loved my touch.
Promise you won’t kneel again
for a love that doesn't stay through the fall.
Promise your eyes won’t search
for someone who’d vanish
like the sun at 5 p.m.
