Everything about you
was early spring wrapped in amber hues.
I was healing when I met you,
and you made me forget I was ever broken.
But maybe I clung too tightly to that warmth.
Maybe I drank too much of you
like the last light before the long night,
and overdosed on what was never mine to keep.

🍂

Because then,
on the tenth of April,
you brought autumn
into my spring.

🍂

The days dimmed,
the colors dulled,
and I watched my joy fall around me
like leaves letting go,
not violently,
but helplessly.
Beautiful in the way endings often are.
Painful in the way they always are.

🍂

And I...
I stood there like a tree in April
still holding on to soft green hope,
when you arrived with wind too cold for the season,
stripping me bare
before I knew how to let go.

🍂

I didn’t ask for the poetry of loss.
I didn’t want the beauty of falling.
I only wanted one more day
under the golden canopy
we built with our hands,
the shade of promises
you no longer meant to keep.
