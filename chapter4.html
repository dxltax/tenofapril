The Season I Still Let In

Sometimes,
I feel unworthy of missing you,
as if longing for you
was a season I was never meant to live through.

I still wonder...
was the warmth you gave me real,
or just the golden haze of autumn
tricking me into thinking
it was still spring?

There are moments
I want to hate you,
for turning me into a character
in a story I didn’t ask to be written into,
for handing me promises
that faded like petals
in the quiet end of bloom.

Sometimes,
you feel like the wind,
cold, sudden,
sweeping through my garden
before it even had the chance to grow.
You didn’t just leave,
you passed through,
and took the light with you.

But those thoughts drift like fallen leaves.
They don’t stay long.
