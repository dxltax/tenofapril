Still Awake in The Season You Left

It’s 2 AM,
and I’m still awake,
just like the countless nights before.

Outside, the wind brushes past the windows,
whispering like the voice I once knew by heart.
The air is colder now,
not from the season alone,
but from your absence that lingers like a fog I can’t outrun.

I still wait for your name to appear on my screen,
like a leaf that refuses to let go of its branch,
hoping, aching,
against the pull of gravity.

I wish you were still the last soul I spoke to before sleep,
and the first light I opened my eyes to at dawn.

But how long can leaves cling
when the wind has made up its mind?
