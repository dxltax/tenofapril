The Memory That Wouldn't Fall

Again,
you slipped into my dreams
like an autumn wind
that won’t stop circling the same dying tree.
I miss you,
or maybe,
I’m just haunted
by the thought of your return.

But whatever it is,
I’m tired of the way God still lets me remember you.
If you were never mine to begin with,
why do you still echo through my mind
like leaves that refuse to fall
even when the sky grows heavy?

They say time heals.
But time, for me, has only deepened the ache.
Each morning I wake
with your shadow pressed against my ribs,
each evening I fall asleep
with the sound of your name rustling through me
like dry leaves no wind can carry away.

You're the shortest season
that ever passed through my life,
and yet the longest grief
I've ever known.

How cruel it is,
to be left by something
so brief,
so beautiful,
so violent in its going.

You left
not like someone walking out the door,
but like autumn itself…
slow at first,
then all at once.

Now all that’s left of you
are colors fading,
air turning colder,
and a silence too loud
to be called peace.
