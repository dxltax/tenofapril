I Am the Tree You Left Behind

How can I forget you
when the world around me
still wears your memory like a season it won’t shed?

Everywhere I turn,
you’re there,
in the hush of golden leaves brushing the pavement,
in the way the wind curls around my skin
like it once curled around your words.

Even the mirror feels heavy now.
I no longer dare to look at my reflection,
not my brows,
not my eyes,
not the curve of my collarbone
you once admired like the outline of early light
on frost-kissed windows.

Tell me,
do those shadows no longer stir something in you?
Do the places you once touched
mean nothing now that they’ve grown cold?
