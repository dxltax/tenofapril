I : If Reincarnation Were Real

If reincarnation were real, I know exactly what I would do with every lifetime the universe grants me. Over and over. In crowded cities or in silent corners of the world, I would search for the sound of your laughter, the weight of your gaze, the quiet way you made everything feel like home.

Even if the universe spun me through a thousand different lives, your name would still be the loudest prayer I carry between each breath. I would choose you, again and again, regardless of the endings fate writes for us—happy, tragic, or somewhere in between.

Because loving you has never been a mistake. Not when the nights were long and cold, not when our memories stung like salt on open wounds. Even when everything fell apart, that love remained—constant, unyielding.

If given just one more chance, one more heartbeat, one more lifetime, I wouldn’t just repeat what we had. I would love you harder. Better. I’d learn your fears before they speak, hold you closer when the world turns away, and never once let doubt nest between us. I would love you so fully that even time itself would forget how to measure the depth of it.

And it wouldn’t matter if we ended in joy or in silence. What we had, what I feel for you, was never about the ending. It was always about the truth of it, the purity of choosing someone even when it hurts.

You are the one soul my love was always meant for. And I would give you everything. Every breath, every moment, every destiny, until there’s nothing left in the universe but the echo of us.

Xo, Delta
