Did the Leaves Ever Whisper My Name?

Since the day you became autumn in my life,
I’ve been asking questions the wind won’t answer.

I still wonder...
was the warmth you gave me real,
or just the golden haze of autumn
tricking me into thinking
it was still spring?

Did you ever wonder how I’d fall
I want to hate you,
after you let go?
How quietly I’d shatter,
like brittle leaves beneath footsteps
that never turned back?

Since you left,
I’ve watched the days grow colder.
I’ve whispered into the spaces
you used to fill.
Did you ever feel even a flicker of guilt
as you drifted away
like a season that never said goodbye?

Did your chest ache
when the skies turned grey,
the way mine does
every time it rains without warning?

I am withering slowly,
like a tree stripped bare
long before the frost has come.
I cry in the hush of midnight
and again beneath the pale morning sun,
but you…
did you feel the same chill
settle into your bones?

No...
I hope not.
