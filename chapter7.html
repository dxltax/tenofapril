Trapped in the Autumn You Left Me

Why does it still feel like this?
Like I’m breathing through fog…
slow, heavy, and cold?

Even when I drown myself in distractions,
you rise above them all,
like fallen leaves that refuse to be swept away.
You’ve become the echo behind every noise,
the shadow behind every corner.
As if the universe has stitched your name
into every thread of my silence.

And what hurts the most,
even your old praises have become wounds.
Once, they made me light.
Now, they weigh me down.

I avoid the mirror,
not because I hate what I see,
but because I see you in the outline of my eyebrows,
in the shape of my eyes,
in the way my collarbones catch the light.
You used to love them.

Do you still?

Or have they lost their magic
now that they no longer belong to your gaze?
